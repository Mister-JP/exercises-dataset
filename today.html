01 · Start

Warm-up

Move first. Save longer held stretches for the end.

1Warm-up

Easy cardio

Treadmill walk, bike, or elliptical—easy enough to speak normally.

3 min gentle pace
  • Let your temperature rise gradually.
  • Keep your breath unhurried; there is nothing to prove.
Pick the easiest available cardio machine and move comfortably for three minutes.
2Warm-up

Shoulder mobility flow

Loosen shoulders and upper back before loaded pressing.

2 min slow and smooth
  • 10 arm circles forward + 10 backward.
  • 10 shoulder rolls backward; 10 hug-and-open swings.
  • 8 slow wall slides, if a wall is nearby.
Keep shoulders away from ears and stay within a completely comfortable range.
3Warm-up

Incline push-up

Use a bench or high Smith-machine bar. This is preparation, not a test.

1 × 6–8 easy effort
  • Keep head, ribs, hips, and heels in one long line.
  • Lower chest to support; exhale as you press away.
  • Shoulders stay away from ears.

02 · Strength

Main workout

Rest 60–90 sec between sets. Start every unfamiliar move with one easy practice set.

4Main

Chest-press machine

Set seat height so handles begin around mid-chest.

2 × 8–10 rest 60–90 sec
  • Feet planted; back supported; shoulder blades gently down and back.
  • Press smoothly without hard elbow lockout.
  • Return in about two seconds—never slam the stack.

5Main

Cable row

Use a standing setup if it feels stable; a seated cable row is an excellent substitute.

2 × 10–12 rest 60–90 sec
  • Stand tall with soft knees and a quiet ribcage.
  • Pull elbows toward ribs; pause as shoulder blades come together.
  • Return under control—do not let the cable pull you forward.

6Main

Seated dumbbell shoulder press

Use a supported bench if available; choose a deliberately light pair.

2 × 8–10 rest 60–90 sec
  • Start with dumbbells beside shoulders, wrists stacked over elbows.
  • Press without low-back arch or upward shrug.
  • Lower to a comfortable shoulder-level range.

7Main

Rope face pull

Set rope around eye/forehead height. Keep this one especially light.

2 × 12–15 rest 60 sec
  • Pull toward eyebrows and let hands separate slightly.
  • Keep neck relaxed and elbows comfortably—not forcefully—high.
  • Think upper-back posture, not shoulders toward ears.

8Main

Ab-crunch machine

Adjust seat so hips stay planted; your abs create the curl, not your neck or arms.

2 × 10–12 rest 60 sec
  • Exhale as ribs curl toward pelvis.
  • Keep hips and feet stable against the machine.
  • Return slowly without fully relaxing the stack.

A small finish, never a requirement.
